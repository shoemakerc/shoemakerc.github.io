Chris Shoemaker: on Music

Introduction
February 16, 2017

I figure that as with any new page that I create in this website, I should give an introduction as to the purposes of this page. I'll do my best to do so, along with an explanation as to why I've created this page.

When I was around 3 years old (being almost 20 years ago the exact details of this story are hazy), my parents placed a cello in my hands, brought me to a private music instructor, and told us to have at it. The results were less than satisfactory: a half hour and at least $50 of my parent's money was spent watching me lay on the floor, flatly refusing (in the style of a 3 year old) to be taught.

I did this twice.

Four years later, my primary school's music program motivated me to try again with the cello and begin learning the instrument in a less rebellious manner. What followed was what has been a fascinating journey, one that has included adding the piano to my repertoire at age 9, being a member of one of the finest youth orchestras in the United States, playing in other numerous fabulous ensembles, and having the privilege of being taught by a number of renowned instructors and performers.

My discovery of music as listener began a bit later than one might think. One of the earliest memories that I still hold is listening to Christy Moore's folk project Planxty and their 1975 compilation The Planxty Collection on a copied CD in my dad's car on a long road trip to Louisville, Kentucky (more on that adventure perhaps later). I guess one could say that this, too, triggered a sequence of one thing leading to another: being fed a steady diet of Beethoven, Mozart, and Nick Cave up to the age of 15, diving into the world of YouTube music videos (a particular favorite of mine), and, finally, jumping into Spotify and its bottomless pit of licensed music.

What I intend for this blog to be is nothing more than a visual to show that music has been, and continues to be, a major part of my life. While this may sound a bit blunt, it is a truth that don't wish to ever deny. Moving forward, I would like to add musical material to this page from different perspectives: as a listener, as a performer, and as a creator. Hopefully I can eventually transcend from this barebones blog look as well (CSS/Bootstrap is still being worked on).

In the meantime, please check out what music I've been listening to recently at the bottom of this page. I update the playlist very frequently, and I hope that perhaps you might pick out a tune there and share my joy of discovery.
